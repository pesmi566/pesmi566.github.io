CINDER

Summary of each chapter

Marissa Meyer — The Lunar Chronicles, Book 1

These summaries are written in my own words (no direct quotes from the novel) and serve as a quick review of each of the 36 chapters. For more detailed analyses (themes, quotes, exam questions), see the first Study Guide document. Marissa Meyer — The Lunar Chronicles, Book 1

Part 1: The Mechanic and the Prince (Chapters 1–8)

Chapter 1

Cinder works as a mechanic in the New Beijing market when a masked young man brings her a damaged android to repair. It gradually becomes clear that he is Prince Kai, traveling incognito. Cinder conceals from him that she is a cyborg.

Chapter 2

At home, Cinder's strained relationship with her stepmother Adri and stepsister Pearl becomes apparent. Only her younger stepsister Peony treats her kindly. Cinder has to hand over her earnings and is exploited as the family's breadwinner.

Chapter 3

Cinder learns more about the rampant Letumosis epidemic and the population's fear of it. The social standing of cyborgs as "inferior" citizens is shown more clearly, as is their fear of being discovered.

Chapter 4

Kai returns to the workshop to pick up the repaired android Nainsi. A first intimacy develops between him and Cinder, although he doesn't suspect that she is a cyborg.

Chapter 5

At the royal palace, Emperor Rikan's serious illness with Letumosis is addressed, increasing the political urgency in the empire. Kai is increasingly taking on responsibility.

Chapter 6

Cinder is publicly humiliated in the marketplace when her mechanical foot becomes visible. The humiliation intensifies her shame about her cyborg body and reveals society's hostility towards cyborgs.

Chapter 7

Cinder and Peony secretly visit a fair near the quarantine area. The tension between curiosity and the real danger of the plague becomes clear.

Chapter 8

After the outing, Peony begins to show symptoms of illness. Cinder's guilt begins to surface, and the family panics at the threat of contagion.

Part 2: The Plague Draws Nearer (Chapters 9–16)

Chapter 9

Peony's illness is confirmed; she is quarantined.

Cinder is distraught, feeling responsible for the infection.

Chapter 10

Adri blames Cinder for Peony's condition and signs her up as a "volunteer" for mandatory cyborg research to combat the epidemic—a punishment with the risk of death.

Chapter 11

Cinder is taken to the palace, where cyborg research is being conducted to develop a cure. She meets the researcher Dr. Erland.

Chapter 12

A test with the pathogen reveals that Cinder is immune to lethuncini—a biologically impossible result that Dr. Erland's interest in her is aroused.

Chapter 13

Dr. Erland begins to examine Cinder more closely and asks unusual questions about her.
