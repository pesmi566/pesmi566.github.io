CINDER

Summary of each chapter

Marissa Meyer — The Lunar Chronicles, Book 1

These summaries are written in my own words (no direct quotes from the novel) and serve as a quick review of each of the 36 chapters. For more detailed analyses (themes, quotes, exam questions), see the first Study Guide document. Marissa Meyer — The Lunar Chronicles, Book 1

Part 1: The Mechanic and the Prince (Chapters 1–8)

Chapter 1

Cinder works as a mechanic in the New Beijing market when a masked young man brings her a damaged android to repair. It gradually becomes clear that he is Prince Kai, traveling incognito. Cinder conceals from him that she is a cyborg.

Chapter 2

At home, Cinder's strained relationship with her stepmother Adri and stepsister Pearl becomes apparent. Only her younger stepsister Peony treats her kindly. Cinder has to hand over her earnings and is exploited as the family's breadwinner.

Chapter 3

Cinder learns more about the rampant Letumosis epidemic and the population's fear of it. The social standing of cyborgs as "inferior" citizens is shown more clearly, as is their fear of being discovered.

Chapter 4

Kai returns to the workshop to pick up the repaired android Nainsi. A first intimacy develops between him and Cinder, although he doesn't suspect that she is a cyborg.

Chapter 5

At the royal palace, Emperor Rikan's serious illness with Letumosis is addressed, increasing the political urgency in the empire. Kai is increasingly taking on responsibility.

Chapter 6

Cinder is publicly humiliated in the marketplace when her mechanical foot becomes visible. The humiliation intensifies her shame about her cyborg body and reveals society's hostility towards cyborgs.

Chapter 7

Cinder and Peony secretly visit a fair near the quarantine area. The tension between curiosity and the real danger of the plague becomes clear.

Chapter 8

After the outing, Peony begins to show symptoms of illness. Cinder's guilt begins to surface, and the family panics at the threat of contagion.

Part 2: The Plague Draws Nearer (Chapters 9–16)

Chapter 9

Peony's illness is confirmed; she is quarantined.

Cinder is distraught, feeling responsible for the infection.

Chapter 10

Adri blames Cinder for Peony's condition and signs her up as a "volunteer" for mandatory cyborg research to combat the epidemic—a punishment with the risk of death.

Chapter 11

Cinder is taken to the palace, where cyborg research is being conducted to develop a cure. She meets the researcher Dr. Erland.

Chapter 12

A test with the pathogen reveals that Cinder is immune to lethuncini—a biologically impossible result that Dr. Erland's interest in her is aroused.

Chapter 13

Dr. Erland begins to examine Cinder more closely and asks unusual questions about her origins and her lack of childhood memories.

Chapter 14

The political situation at court intensifies: Queen Levana of Luna announces her arrival, causing unrest at court.

Chapter 15

Kai and Cinder meet again; Her affection grows as Cinder continues to conceal her true identity.

Chapter 17

Cinder briefly returns home and experiences the despair of seeing Peony's deteriorating condition.

Part 3: Loss and Political Pressure (Chapters 18–24)

Chapter 18

Peony dies from the plague. Her death is an emotional turning point that deeply shakes Cinder and deepens her isolation.

Queen Levana arrives on Earth. Her intimidating presence and her ability to manipulate (glamour) are felt at court.

Chapter 20

Levana pressures Kai to marry her to avert an impending war between Luna and the Commonwealth.

Chapter 21

Emperor Rikan's health continues to deteriorate. Kai takes on more and more governmental responsibilities and comes under increasing pressure.

Chapter 22

Cinder continues to work secretly with Dr. Erland and learns more about the Lunar abilities and their dangers.

Kai invites everyone to a ball to ease political tensions; Cinder is also invited, which throws her into an inner conflict.

Chapter 24

Cinder's feelings for Kai deepen, while at the same time she knows she owes him the truth about herself and about Levana.

Part 4: The Secret Revealed (Chapters 25–30)

Chapter 25

Emperor Rikan dies from the plague. Kai is crowned the new emperor and now bears full political responsibility.

Chapter 26

Dr. Erland hints to Cinder that she might be the supposedly dead Lunar princess Selene – the rightful heir to the Luna throne.

Chapter 27

Cinder struggles with this revelation and with the fear of what this truth would mean for her life and her relationship with Kai.

Chapter 28

Levana continues to push for the wedding with Kai and openly threatens an attack on Earth should he refuse.

Chapter 29

Cinder decides to warn Kai about Levana's true nature at the upcoming ball, even at the risk of revealing her own identity.

Chapter 30

Preparations for the ball are underway; Cinder prepares herself inwardly for the confrontation and weighs the risks of her revelation.

Part 5: The Ball and the Escape (Chapters 31–36)

Chapter 31

Cinder appears at the ball to warn Kai—a scene reminiscent of the Cinderella motif, but politically charged.

Chapter 32

Her attempt to tell Kai the truth is complicated by Levana's influence and the general confusion.

Chapter 33

Cinder's identity as a cyborg—and consequently her connection to Luna—is revealed before the assembled court. exposed.

Chapter 34

In the panic of being exposed, Cinder flees; during her escape, she loses her mechanical foot—a reimagining of the glass slipper.

Chapter 35

Cinder is captured and imprisoned. Despite the hopeless situation, she begins to develop an escape plan using her mechanical abilities.

Chapter 37

With the help of Dr. Erland, Cinder manages to escape from prison. The novel ends on an open note: She now knows her true identity, but the fate of the Commonwealth and her relationship with Kai remain unresolved.

Chapter 38

In the short final chapter, the cliffhanger is confirmed: Cinder is on the run, Levana's threat still hangs in the air, and the plot clearly sets the stage for the continuation of the series.
